Excellent work. I think we've reached an important realization that changes the direction of the product.

I no longer want to design Job Mode around "phases" or "forms."

I want to design it around **Job States** and **Activities**.

This is a fundamental product philosophy change.

Do not write production code yet.

I want to redesign the experience around this concept first.

---

# New Product Philosophy

UnitDown does not ask technicians to document their work.

UnitDown quietly observes the job, remembers everything important, and automatically builds the industry's most trusted service record while the technician simply repairs equipment.

Documentation is no longer the goal.

Memory is the goal.

The report becomes a byproduct.

---

# Think Like an Experienced Apprentice

The app should feel like a senior technician standing beside you.

It never interrupts.

It never overwhelms.

It simply remembers everything and occasionally asks the next logical question.

The technician should never ask:

"What do I need to document?"

Instead the app should quietly know.

---

# Replace "Phases" With "Job States"

Technicians never think:

"I'm entering Phase 6."

They think:

"I found the problem."

"I replaced the belt."

"I'm checking my readings."

The UI should reflect that.

Example Job States:

Job Created

↓

Arrived

↓

Equipment Verified

↓

Initial Observation

↓

Measurements Captured

↓

Root Cause Identified

↓

Repair Started

↓

Repair Completed

↓

Verification Complete

↓

Recommendations Added

↓

Customer Reviewed

↓

USR Generated

Each state unlocks the next intelligent suggestion.

The technician progresses naturally through the repair.

---

# Replace "Events" With "Activities"

Internally they may still be timeline events.

But the technician should simply see:

Job Activity

Examples:

🟢 Arrived

📷 Nameplate Captured

⚡ Alarm 82

📈 Measurements Recorded

🎤 Voice Note

🔧 Belt Installed

📷 Repair Photo

✅ Verification Complete

Activities feel natural.

The timeline becomes the story of the repair.

---

# One Question At A Time

Never overwhelm the technician.

Instead of showing long checklists...

Ask one intelligent question.

Example:

✓ Belt Installed

What's next?

○ Measure motor amps

○ Take repair photo

○ Verify rotation

○ Nothing else

One decision.

Continue working.

---

# The Timeline Is Equipment Memory

The timeline should not exist simply to build reports.

It should become permanent equipment memory.

Every completed activity adds to that equipment's long-term intelligence.

Examples:

Recurring failures

Parts history

Warranty history

Seasonal behavior

Ignored recommendations

Previous measurements

Known weak components

Every service call makes the equipment smarter.

---

# Job Mode Is A Flight Recorder

This is an important philosophy.

Do not design Job Mode like a note-taking application.

Design it like a flight recorder.

The system automatically records everything important.

The technician only adds information when necessary.

Everything else should happen automatically whenever possible.

Examples:

Arrival timestamp

Weather

GPS

Photos

Voice

Measurements

Timeline

Recommendations

Repair verification

All captured with minimal friction.

---

# Reduce Cognitive Load

The technician should focus on repairing equipment.

Not operating software.

If a decision can be inferred...

Infer it.

If AI can categorize something...

Categorize it.

If OCR can read it...

Read it.

If history already exists...

Reuse it.

If the next step is obvious...

Suggest it.
