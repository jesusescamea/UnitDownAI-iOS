Implement the following improvements to the Dispatch Dashboard prototype. These are UX enhancements only. Preserve the existing UnitDown design language, dark theme, spacing, animations, and performance.

====================================================
1. JOB CARD HIERARCHY
====================================================

The customer/business location should always be the primary piece of information.

Current:

Carrier 50XCQ006 – RTU-3
Summit Medical Plaza

Replace with:

Summit Medical Plaza
North Roof • RTU-3
Carrier 50XCQ006

Reason:
Technicians think:
"Where am I going?"
before
"What unit am I working on?"

Business/location should be the largest and boldest text.

----------------------------------------------------

====================================================
2. PROFILE ICON
====================================================

The initials avatar (MR) should no longer be decorative.

Make it fully clickable.

Open an Account Dashboard containing:

• Profile
• My Account
• Subscription
• Today's Statistics
• Hours Worked
• Completed Jobs
• Active Jobs
• PM Completion %
• Follow-ups
• Saved Equipment
• Inventory
• Settings
• Offline Mode
• Help & Support
• Logout

Eventually this becomes the technician's personal hub.

----------------------------------------------------

====================================================
3. OVERVIEW CARDS
====================================================

The Overview cards should function as navigation shortcuts.

Today's Jobs
→ opens today's assignments

In Progress
→ opens active jobs

PM Due
→ opens PM schedule

Follow-ups
→ opens pending callbacks, quoted repairs, customer approvals, unresolved recommendations

Each card should open a filtered dashboard instead of only displaying numbers.

----------------------------------------------------

====================================================
4. CALENDAR
====================================================

Keep the compact monthly calendar.

It should become the technician's timeline.

Each day should display colored indicators.

Orange = Service Call

Blue = PM

Red = Emergency

Green = Completed

Yellow = Follow-up

Gray = No activity

Selecting a day filters the dashboard to that day's activity.

Future enhancement:

Small indicator numbers showing job count per day.

----------------------------------------------------

====================================================
5. EQUIPMENT INTELLIGENCE
====================================================

This should become UnitDown's flagship feature.

Instead of simply displaying previous repairs...

The AI should analyze repair history and infer likely root causes.

Example:

Repeated Code 82

Current:

Repeated Code 82

Replace with:

Repeated Code 82

3 lockouts
3 visits
2 condenser cleanings

AI Insight

Repeated condenser cleaning has not resolved the issue.

Possible root causes:

• Condenser airflow restriction
• Incorrect fan operation
• Overcharge
• Non-condensables
• Liquid line restriction

Another example:

3 belt replacements in 2 months

AI Insight

Repeated belt failures are rarely caused by the belt itself.

Inspect:

• Pulley alignment
• Worn sheaves
• Motor bearings
• Shaft runout
• Motor mounting
• Belt tension

Another:

TXV replaced twice

AI Insight

The history suggests the TXV may not be the root problem.

Investigate:

• Moisture contamination
• Liquid restriction
• Poor evacuation
• Improper sensing bulb installation
• Refrigerant contamination

The AI should continuously compare previous visits and formulate the most probable underlying issue before the technician even starts diagnostics.

----------------------------------------------------

====================================================

====================================================

Every Equipment Intelligence card should be clickable.

Opening one displays:

• Full service history
• Timeline
• Previous repairs
• AI reasoning
• Technician notes
• Measurements
• Alarm history
• Parts replaced
• Trends
• Predicted next failure
• Launch Diagnostic button

This becomes the "brain" of UnitDown.

----------------------------------------------------

====================================================
7. MESSAGES
====================================================

Messages should not live at the bottom where technicians may overlook them.

Instead...

When Start Job is pressed...

If dispatch notes exist...

Show a required Job Brief popup.

Example:

Before you leave...

Dispatch Notes

• Badge required

• Customer requested arrival after 1 PM

• Gate code changed

• Roof hatch unlocked

• Previous technician reported recurring Code 82

Technician taps:

✓ Continue

Only display this popup when important notes exist.

Do not interrupt the workflow unnecessarily.

----------------------------------------------------

====================================================
9. DASHBOARD WITH NO SCHEDULED JOBS
====================================================

When a technician has no jobs scheduled, do not display an empty dashboard.

Instead show a productivity dashboard featuring:

• Compact monthly calendar
• Follow-ups
• Equipment Intelligence
• Saved equipment
• PMs due this week
• Recent activity
• Open recommendations
• AI insights
• Training reminders
• Equipment needing attention

This keeps the app useful every day, even without active dispatches.

----------------------------------------------------

====================================================
10. OVERALL DESIGN GOAL
====================================================

Shift UnitDown from being perceived as "an AI diagnostic app" to becoming a complete Commercial HVAC Field Operating System.

The dashboard should feel like the command center for a commercial service technician, where dispatch, history, predictive maintenance, AI diagnostics, customer information, PM tracking, follow-ups, equipment intelligence, and technician workflow all come together in one place.

The interface should immediately answer:

• Where am I going?
• What needs attention?
• What has happened before?
• What should I bring?
• What should I check first?
• What can I finish today?

Build this to feel polished, premium, and production-ready while maintaining the current UnitDown design language.
