# Job Mode UX Refactor — Technician Workflow First

Do NOT add new backend architecture.

Do NOT modify the database schema.

Do NOT change the Job Timeline architecture.

Do NOT change the offline queue.

Do NOT change USS.

This is strictly a **user experience refactor**.

---

# Problem

Job Mode currently behaves like a separate module.

Technicians do not think:

"I'm going to Job Mode."

They think:

"I'm going to Sunrise Medical Center."

Then:

"I'm working on RTU-3."

Job Mode should naturally begin from the equipment they are already viewing.

The current navigation introduces unnecessary friction.

---

# New Workflow

Field Hub

↓

Customer / Site

↓

Equipment

↓

START JOB

↓

Job Mode

↓

Complete Job

↓

UnitDown Service Record

The technician should never need to hunt for Job Mode.

---

# 1. Customer Card

Every Customer/Site card should include:

▶ Start Job

If a job already exists:

▶ Resume Job

Starting from the customer automatically carries:

Customer

Site

Technician

Date

into the Job Session.

---

# 2. Equipment Detail

The Equipment Detail page becomes the primary place technicians begin work.

Near:

Start Diagnosis

Add:

Start Job

If a job already exists:

Resume Job

This should become one of the most visible actions on the page.

---

# 3. Active Job Banner

While a job is active, display a persistent banner throughout the app.

Example:

ACTIVE JOB

Sunrise Medical Center

RTU-3

01:14 elapsed

Resume →

This banner should appear regardless of where the technician navigates.

The technician should always be one tap away from returning to the active job.

---

# 4. Floating Resume Button

If an active job exists:

Show a floating Resume button.

This replaces the need to navigate back through menus.

---

# 5. Job Mode Navigation

Do NOT remove the standalone Job Mode page.

Instead rename its purpose.

Current:

Job Mode

New purpose:

Active Jobs

This page becomes a dashboard.

Contents:

Current Job

Draft Jobs

Offline Jobs

Pending Sync

Completed Today

Resume buttons

It is NOT where technicians start work.

It is where they manage active work.

---

# 6. Auto Navigation

After pressing:

Start Job

Automatically:

Open Job Mode.

No intermediate screens.

No extra confirmation.

Begin immediately.

---

# 7. Resume Logic

If a technician taps:

Start Job

while an unfinished job already exists for that equipment:

Prompt:

Resume existing job?

Start new job?

This prevents accidental duplicates.

---

# 8. Equipment Context

When Job Mode opens it should already know:

Customer

Site

Equipment

Equipment type

Manufacturer

Model

Serial

Existing recommendations

Existing Equipment Memory

Recent service history

The technician should never re-enter information already available.

---

# 9. Completion Flow

When Complete Job is pressed:

Automatically:

Generate USS record

Generate USR ID

Generate Service Record

Navigate directly to the completed Service Record.

Do NOT return the technician to Job Mode.

---

# 10. Remove Navigation Friction

A technician should be able to begin documenting work in two taps:

Customer

↓

Start Job

or

Equipment

↓

Start Job

No additional setup screens.

---

# 11. UX Philosophy

Design Job Mode around how technicians actually think.

They think in:

Customers

Buildings

Equipment

Problems

Repairs

NOT:

Jobs

Modules

Workspaces

The app should adapt to the technician—not the other way around.

---

# Deliverables

Return:

• Updated navigation flow

• Files modified

• Components changed

• Screenshots

• Typecheck results

Do NOT modify backend architecture.

Do NOT modify USS.

Do NOT modify offline architecture.

This task is focused entirely on making Job Mode feel like a seamless extension of the equipment workflow rather than a separate feature.
