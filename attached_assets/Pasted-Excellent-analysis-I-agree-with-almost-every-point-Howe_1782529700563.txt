Excellent analysis. I agree with almost every point.

However, I think we're still designing individual features instead of designing the technician's complete workflow.

I want to step back before building more screens.

Job Mode should not feel like another form.

It should feel like carrying an experienced apprentice that remembers everything.

## New design philosophy

The technician should almost never ask:

"What do I need to document?"

Instead the app should constantly observe the job and quietly help.

Every interaction should naturally create documentation.

The technician simply works.

The report writes itself.

---

## The new priority order

### Priority 1 (Highest)

Design the complete lifecycle of one service call.

From:

Dispatch Received

↓

Driving

↓

Arrival

↓

Diagnosis

↓

Measurements

↓

Repair

↓

Verification

↓

Recommendations

↓

Customer discussion

↓

Complete Job

↓

AI Review

↓

USR Generated

↓

Office Ready

↓

Equipment Memory Updated

↓

Ready for next call

Do NOT design isolated screens.

Design one continuous experience.

---

### Priority 2

Every major event should intelligently unlock the next one.

Examples:

Nameplate captured

↓

Suggest alarm capture

Alarm captured

↓

Suggest initial measurements

High head pressure found

↓

Suggest condenser inspection

Belt replacement detected

↓

Suggest:

• Belt size

• Pulley diameter

• Motor amps

• Rotation

Filter replacement

↓

Suggest filter dimensions

Filter type

MERV

Quantity

Location

The technician should never have to remember documentation requirements.

---

### Priority 3

Design the fastest capture flow for every data type.

For each one answer:

How many taps?

Can voice replace typing?

Can AI infer the information?

Can the camera identify it?

Can the app suggest it automatically?

Target:

Most documentation should require zero or one additional tap.

---

### Priority 4

The app must feel alive.

Examples:

"Photo saved."

"Offline — still saving."

"Recovered after reconnect."

"AI identified this as a nameplate."

"Looks like a blower belt."

"You already replaced this belt 14 months ago."

"Customer has had this alarm 3 previous visits."

The technician should constantly feel assisted, not questioned.

---

### Priority 5

Design the emotional moments.

Today we only designed the work.

We have not designed the moments technicians actually remember.

Examples:

• Starting the job.

• Discovering the root cause.

• Completing the repair.

• AI building the report.

• USR generation.

• Office Ready confirmation.

Those moments should feel satisfying and memorable.

---

### Priority 6

Think bigger than documentation.

The end goal is not simply producing a report.

The end goal is creating the industry's trusted service record.

One day customers, manufacturers, contractors, facility managers, warranty departments, and future technicians should actively ask:

"Do you have the UnitDown Service Record for this equipment?"

Every design decision should move toward making the UnitDown Service Record the trusted standard for documenting commercial HVAC service.

---

Before building another production component, I want to map the entire technician journey as one connected experience, identify every friction point, and then build each workflow in that order.

Workflow first.

Screens second.

Visual polish last.
