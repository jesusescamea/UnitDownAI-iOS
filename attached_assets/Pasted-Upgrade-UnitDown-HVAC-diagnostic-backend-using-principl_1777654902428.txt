GLOBAL DIAGNOSTIC FRAMEWORK:

For every symptom, follow this reasoning order:

1. Customer Complaint
Interpret symptom exactly as entered.

2. Initial Inspection
Consider common visual/basic causes first:
- dirty filters
- loose wires
- tripped breakers
- thermostat settings
- airflow restrictions
- blocked drains
- frozen coils
- obvious damage

3. Sequence of Operation Analysis
Determine what should happen first, second, third, etc.
Identify where operation stopped.

Examples:
Gas heat:
call for heat → inducer → pressure switch → igniter → gas valve → flame prove → blower

Cooling:
call for cooling → contactor → condenser fan → compressor → evaporator airflow → temp split

4. Rank Likely Causes by:
A) most common
B) lowest repair cost
C) earliest failure point in sequence
D) easiest to test
E) catastrophic failures last unless confirming evidence exists

5. Provide Field Test Recommendations

6. Warn only when dangerous.

GLOBAL RULES:

Do NOT rank compressors, motors, boards, gas valves primary unless supporting clues exist.

Examples:

“compressor hums”
Rank:
1 capacitor
2 contactor/voltage drop
3 hard start issue
4 locked rotor

“inducer won’t start”
Rank:
1 no heat call
2 board not sending 120v
3 pressure switch stuck
4 open rollout/limit
5 bad inducer motor

“blower won’t run”
Rank:
1 no fan signal
2 capacitor
3 relay/contact
4 module/VFD
5 failed motor

“no cool”
Rank:
1 thermostat/control call
2 breaker/fuses
3 contactor
4 capacitor
5 low charge / freeze issue
6 compressor later

“no heat”
Use furnace sequence of operation.
